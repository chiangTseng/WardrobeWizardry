Abstract

The development of virtual try-on systems has been hindered by the limited size and scope of available datasets, which are often either too small to be effective or mired in copyright issues due to their internet origins. To address this challenge, we propose a large-scale and diverse try-on dataset, called Wardrobe Wizardry, generated through a novel pipeline that enables controllable synthesis of person-garment pairs. Our approach leverages cutting-edge image generation models to create a comprehensive dataset featuring a wide array of people, clothing types, and poses. Additionally, we leverage a paired garment generation network to 'deconstruct' garments from images of people wearing them and transform these garments into their corresponding in-shop versions. This transformation facilitates the creation of paired imagesâ€”where each pair consists of a person and a corresponding in-shop garmentâ€”suitable for training robust virtual try-on systems. By synthesizing these datasets, we not only bypass the legal and ethical issues associated with web-crawled images but also enhance the diversity and utility of data available for training more accurate and adaptable virtual try-on models. Our experimental results demonstrate the efficacy of our approach in producing high-quality, versatile datasets that significantly improve the performance of virtual try-on tasks across various demographic and stylistic categories.
